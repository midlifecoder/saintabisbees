Intro to Beekeeping

This class is for anyone considering keeping bees. The class will provide a basic understanding of what is needed to keep bees, how/where to get your bees, the beekeeper’s calendar, the cost of keeping bees, and the local associations that support our beekeepers.
Sign up begins September 2023.
Classes begin January 2024.

Beekeeping 101 – The First Year

This class is for anyone who plans to keep bees or already has bees but would like to learn more! The class will cover the history of beekeeping, beekeeping equipment, beehive styles and their uses, honeybee biology, how/where to acquire bees, installing your bees, hive inspections, swarms, summer maintenance, honey extraction, pest and disease management, winterization, local associations, and the beekeeper’s calendar. This class will also offer 3 additional field days for some extra hands-on time with the bees.
Sign up begins October 2023.
Classes begin February 2024.

Field Days

Field days are for anyone who would like hands on time with the bees and who would like to learn more about keeping bees all year round. We offer one field day a month from early spring to early summer, then we pause during the summer heat, and then come back in late summer and end in the early fall. Each field day we will tackle a specific inspection goal based on the time of year, interpret what we find in the hive, and create a plan for success until the next inspection.
Sign up begins October 2023.
Field Days begin March 2024.

Beekeeping Consultation

Most “new bees” just need a helping hand to build knowledge as they work their hives. Let us partner with you, we love helping established beekeepers be successful! Our consultations are designed to be intermittent as opposed to ongoing but can be monthly if desired. Our goal is to reduce your confusion and increase your confidence through education and mentorship!

Kimberlee

Queen Bee

I became interested in beekeeping in 2012 after picking up a book I thought would pass the time on a long plane trip. The book wasn’t overly scientific or technical, it was just an everyday woman talking about her experience as a beekeeper. I was intrigued and thought, ‘if she can do it, then why can’t I?’. It’s funny, because when something is right, it’s just right. I knew as soon as I put those first ladies into their new hive that I was hooked and since then, I haven’t looked back! As the years have passed, I have been blessed to have my best “drone” by my side as we continue this journey in beekeeping. I am thankful that the bees allow us to be a small part of their world and I hope you find as much enjoyment as we do spending time with them!

Saint Abigail

Patron Saint Of Beekeepers – Celebrated February 11th

Saint Abigail was a medieval Irish saint born around the 6th century in Ireland. According to Celtic folklore, when the soul departs from the body it takes the form of a bee or butterfly. So, it is not surprising that Abigail also became a beekeeper. It is said that she developed a powerful relationship with the bees and would use their honey to treat illnesses and heal wounds. She became known for her miracles in rousting bees from their hives and using them to chase off evil. Like Saint Abi, may your bees bring you health, happiness, and peace!
